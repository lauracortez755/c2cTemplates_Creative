Gentrification in the Austin Area

We examine the role of gentrification in the local Austin Area and the implications it has for our communities.

Find Out More

About

Gentrification is defined as the process of renovating and improving a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste. Yet for many minority communities across the Austin Area, a mere definition does little justice. In fact, justice in these regions is often hard to come by, as poorer people are forced out of their homes enabling the influx of white middle class citizens. The role of our team is to help combat this issue through raising awareness and promoting ethical takes and arguments over the issue. We are here to inform the mass of the issue that overtakes our neighborhoods so that those with resources can do it justice that we could only imagine to accomplish.

Defining Issues

Less Minority Populations

The process of gentrification pushes out minority populations due to inflation of rent prices and businesses opening that cater to white middle-class tastes.

Less Cultural Diversity

Gentrification causes mom and pop shops to foreclose and be replaced with corporations like Whole Foods and Starbucks, lessening the cultural uniqueness.

Perpetuates Capitalism

Gentrification is a capitalist process that favors the wealthier classes and supports the growth of commercial corporations.

Favors Middle Class

Most of the "improvements" are not meant to help the populations already living there, but to be appealing to the middle class.
